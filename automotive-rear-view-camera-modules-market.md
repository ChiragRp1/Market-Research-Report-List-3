<p><p>CCD (charge-coupled device) cameras and CMOS (complementary metal-oxide semiconductor) cameras are two common types of rear view camera modules in the automotive market. CCD cameras offer high-quality images with better light sensitivity and lower noise levels, making them suitable for low-light conditions. On the other hand, CMOS cameras are more cost-effective, consume less power, and provide faster image processing capabilities. Both types of cameras play a crucial role in enhancing driver visibility and safety while reversing or maneuvering the vehicle.</p></p>
