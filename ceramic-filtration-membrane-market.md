<p><p>Ceramic filtration membranes are classified into three main types based on the pore size and filtration efficiency: ultrafiltration, microfiltration, and nanofiltration. </p><p>Ultrafiltration membranes have larger pore sizes that can separate particles, macromolecules, and colloids from liquids. Microfiltration membranes have smaller pores that can remove bacteria and suspended solids. Nanofiltration membranes have even smaller pores that can remove divalent ions and organic compounds.</p><p>Each type of membrane is used for specific applications based on the size of particles that need to be filtered.</p></p>
