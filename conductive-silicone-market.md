<p><p>Conductive silicone is categorized into three main types based on its form and consistency: Elastomers, Resins, and Gels. Elastomers are rubber-like materials that offer flexibility and resilience, making them suitable for applications requiring stretchability and durability. Resins are solid materials that provide stability and structural support, commonly used in manufacturing processes. Gels are semi-solid materials with a soft texture, ideal for sealing and bonding applications. Each type of conductive silicone offers unique properties and characteristics for various industry needs.</p></p>
