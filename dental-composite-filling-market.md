<p><ul><li>Resin-based Composites</li><li>Poly-acid Modified Composites</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental composite filling market has two main types: resin-based composites and poly-acid modified composites. Resin-based composites consist of a mixture of organic resins and inorganic particles, providing excellent aesthetic results and durability. They are commonly used for the restoration of anterior and posterior teeth. Poly-acid modified composites, also known as glass ionomer cements, are made from a combination of acrylic resins and fluoroaluminosilicate glass, offering improved strength and adhesion. They are often used for restorations in areas with high moisture levels or in pediatric dentistry.</p></p>
