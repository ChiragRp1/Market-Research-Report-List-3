<p><p>In the Japanese Shochu market, there are two main types of production methods: Multiply Distilled and Singly Distilled. Multiply Distilled Shochu is made by distilling the alcohol multiple times, resulting in a more refined and smooth taste. This type of Shochu is often more expensive and considered higher quality. On the other hand, Singly Distilled Shochu is only distilled once, resulting in a stronger and more robust flavor profile. Singly Distilled Shochu is typically more affordable and appeals to those who prefer a bolder taste.</p></p>
