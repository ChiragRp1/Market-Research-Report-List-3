<p><p>Large-area LCD displays can be divided into three main types: static, simple matrix, and active matrix. Static displays use a fixed array of pixels to display images or information, suitable for basic purposes. Simple matrix displays have rows and columns of pixels that can switch on and off to create images, and are typically used for signs and billboards. Active matrix displays have thin film transistors on each pixel, allowing for fast switching and high resolution, commonly found in TVs, computer monitors, and smartphones.</p></p>
