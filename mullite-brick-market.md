<p><p>Mullite bricks are commonly divided into two main types: sintered mullite bricks and fused mullite bricks. Sintered mullite bricks are formed by heating a mixture of alumina and silica at high temperatures, while fused mullite bricks are made by melting alumina and silica together. Both types of mullite bricks are known for their high refractoriness, thermal shock resistance, and excellent thermal insulation properties, making them popular choices for applications in industries such as steel, ceramics, and petrochemicals.</p></p>
