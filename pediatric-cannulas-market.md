<p><p>The pediatric cannulas market includes three different types of cannulas: venous cannulas, arterial cannulas, and high flow nasal cannulas. Venous cannulas are used to deliver medication or fluids directly into the veins of children. Arterial cannulas are used to measure blood pressure or collect blood samples from the arteries of pediatric patients. High flow nasal cannulas are used to provide oxygen, humidification, and controlled airflow to the nasal passages of children with respiratory conditions. These different types of cannulas serve specific purposes in pediatric medical care.</p></p>
