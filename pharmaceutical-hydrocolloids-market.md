<p><ul><li>Seaweed Hydrocolloids</li><li>Arabic Gum</li><li>Carrageenan and Agar</li><li>Xanthan Gum</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Pharmaceutical hydrocolloids are classified into four main types: Seaweed hydrocolloids, Arabic gum, Carrageenan and Agar, and Xanthan gum. Seaweed hydrocolloids are derived from seaweed plants and are used as thickening and stabilizing agents in pharmaceutical products. Arabic gum is a natural gum derived from the Acacia Senegal tree and is commonly used in tablets and coatings. Carrageenan and Agar are extracted from red seaweed and are used as gelling agents in pharmaceutical formulations. Xanthan gum is a microbial polysaccharide that is widely used as a stabilizer and thickening agent in pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
