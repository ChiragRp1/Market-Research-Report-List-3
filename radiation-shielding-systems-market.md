<p><p>Radiation shielding systems are designed to protect individuals and the environment from harmful radiation exposure. There are two main types of radiation: indirectly ionizing radiation, which includes gamma rays and X-rays, and directly ionizing radiation, which includes alpha and beta particles. Indirectly ionizing radiation requires thicker shielding materials such as lead or concrete, while directly ionizing radiation can be effectively stopped by lighter materials like plastic or aluminum. Both types of shielding systems play a crucial role in ensuring safety in medical, industrial, and environmental settings.</p></p>
