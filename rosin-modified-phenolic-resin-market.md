<p><p>Rosin Modified Phenolic Resins are classified into two main types based on their application methods - Dissolving Type and Cooking Type. Dissolving Type resins are typically dissolved in organic solvents before being applied to substrates, making them suitable for use in coatings, adhesives, and inks. On the other hand, Cooking Type resins are melted and mixed with other ingredients at high temperatures, commonly used in the manufacturing of plywood, laminates, and plastics. Both types offer unique characteristics and performance benefits in various industries.</p></p>
