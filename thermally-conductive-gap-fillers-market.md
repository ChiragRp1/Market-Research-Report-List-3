<p><p>Thermally conductive gap fillers are used to transfer heat away from electronic components to prevent overheating. Silicone thermally conductive gap fillers comprise a silicone base with added thermally conductive fillers, providing flexibility and good thermal conductivity. Non-silicone thermally conductive gap fillers are made from materials like acrylic or polyurethane, offering high thermal conductivity and stability over a wide temperature range. Both types cater to different applications and requirements in industries like electronics, automotive, and aerospace.</p></p>
