<p><p>Thermosetting resin polymerization inhibitors can be classified into two main types: molecular type and stable radical type. Molecular inhibitors work by reacting with free radicals to halt the polymerization process. Stable radical inhibitors, on the other hand, form long-lived radicals that disrupt the polymerization reaction. Both types play a crucial role in controlling the curing process of thermosetting resins, ensuring the desired properties and performance of the final product. These inhibitors are essential components in industries such as composites, adhesives, and coatings.</p></p>
