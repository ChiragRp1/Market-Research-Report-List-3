<p><p>Vendor Management Systems (VMS) are software platforms that help organizations manage their relationships with vendors. There are two main types of VMS markets: On-Premise and Cloud-Based. On-Premise VMS refers to software installed and hosted on the organization's own servers, allowing for full control and customization but requiring maintenance and IT support. Cloud-Based VMS, on the other hand, operates on remote servers and is accessed through the internet. It offers scalability, accessibility, and often lower upfront costs since it eliminates the need for hardware and infrastructure maintenance. Both options have pros and cons based on an organization's specific needs and resources.</p></p>
